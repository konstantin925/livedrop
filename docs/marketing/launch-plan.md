# LiveDrop Launch Plan

## Goal

Launch LiveDrop with a practical, low-cost growth plan that matches the current product: mobile-first browsing, nearby live deals, online deal discovery, and Drop Mode for sharper urgency.

## Brand Guardrails

- Exciting, not loud
- Premium, not corporate
- Urgent, not spammy
- Mobile-first, fast, clear, swipeable
- Focus on real value: live deals, limited-time drops, nearby discovery

## Core Positioning

LiveDrop helps people catch live local and online deals before they disappear.

## Messaging Pillars

1. Real-time value
   Live deals near you and online, not stale coupon clutter.
2. Fast discovery
   Open the app, scan what is live now, act before it is gone.
3. Premium urgency
   Drop Mode surfaces the sharpest limited-time opportunities without sounding chaotic.
4. Local business upside
   Businesses get immediate visibility for timely offers without needing a large ad budget.

## Launch-Ready Messaging

### Consumer one-liners

- Catch the drop before it is gone.
- Live deals near you, plus online drops worth tapping fast.
- Skip coupon clutter. See what is live right now.
- The fastest way to spot nearby deals and limited-time online drops.

### Consumer short app store / bio copy

LiveDrop helps you find live local deals and limited-time online drops in one fast mobile feed. Open the app, see what is live now, and grab the best value before it disappears.

### Business one-liners

- Turn slow hours into live demand.
- Drop a deal. Bring people in now.
- Timed offers for local businesses that want action, not wasted reach.

## Audience Priorities

### Early consumer audiences

- College students and young professionals
- People already using TikTok and Instagram for food, coffee, shopping, and local finds
- Users who like deals but dislike low-quality coupon sites

### Early business audiences

- Coffee shops
- Fast casual and dessert spots
- Fitness studios
- Independent retail
- Local businesses with quiet windows and expiring inventory

## Channel Strategy

### TikTok

Role: top-of-funnel awareness and creator-style proof

What to post:

- Screen-recording walkthroughs of live nearby deals
- Fast "best drops today" roundups
- Before/after value clips: normal price vs live drop
- Local neighborhood deal runs

Format:

- 12 to 25 seconds
- Hook in first 2 seconds
- On-screen text heavy
- Native phone capture is fine

Posting cadence:

- 2 to 3 posts per day during launch week
- 1 to 2 posts per day after

Sample hooks:

- I checked LiveDrop and found 3 deals near me in 2 minutes
- This is how I find limited-time food deals without digging through coupon sites
- Drop Mode just served me the best online deal of the day
- If your city has hidden deals, this is how to catch them live

Creator angles:

- Budget food creator: best under-$10 local stops
- Student lifestyle creator: cheap coffee, fast lunch, last-minute deals
- Local city creator: what is actually worth grabbing today
- Tech/deal creator: online drops and flash-value finds

CTA:

- Download LiveDrop and check what is live near you
- Open Drop Mode and watch the feed tighten up

### Instagram

Role: polished trust, repostable local discovery, story engagement

What to post:

- Reels adapted from TikTok
- Story polls: "Would you claim this?"
- Carousel: 5 live deals in one neighborhood
- Short founder-style clips explaining how to use the app in under 15 seconds

Posting cadence:

- 1 reel per day
- 5 to 10 stories per week
- 2 carousels per week

Creative direction:

- Clean captions
- Minimal overlays
- Premium color treatment
- Avoid cluttered coupon aesthetics

CTA:

- Check today’s live drops
- Send this to a friend in your city

### Reddit

Role: credibility, city-by-city awareness, feedback, and local traction

Target communities:

- City subreddits
- Food and frugal communities where self-promotion is allowed
- Small business and local entrepreneurship communities for merchant-side messaging

Approach:

- Lead with usefulness, not promotion
- Post city-specific value summaries
- Share real examples of deals spotted in the app
- Ask for feedback on what kinds of local drops people want most

Reddit post angles:

- I built a cleaner way to find live local deals without coupon spam. Looking for feedback.
- We are testing a local deal feed for [City]. What kinds of drops would actually make you open it daily?
- Small business owners: would you use timed local offers to fill slow hours?

Guardrail:

- No generic launch spam
- Every post should include a useful prompt, real screenshot, or local insight

### Local Business Growth

Role: supply-side growth and retention

Primary acquisition path:

- Founder-led outbound DM and in-person visits
- Focus on one city or neighborhood cluster first

Who to contact first:

- Businesses that already run daily specials
- Shops with slow mid-day or late-evening windows
- Places that photograph well for social proof

Offer:

- Free pilot for first wave of merchants
- Concierge onboarding: "We will help you post your first live drop"
- Simple promise: more visibility during low-traffic windows

Outreach script:

Hi, I am working on LiveDrop, a mobile app for nearby live deals and time-sensitive drops. We are onboarding a small first group of local businesses and helping them post limited-time offers that can drive same-day traffic. If you want, I can set up your first drop with you in a few minutes and keep it simple.

Retention motion:

- Send a weekly performance summary manually at first
- Ask merchants which time windows need traffic most
- Turn winning drops into case-study screenshots

## Launch Content Pack

### 5 short social captions

1. Live deals move fast. That is the point.
2. Nearby deals. Online drops. No coupon-site chaos.
3. Open LiveDrop. See what is live. Move early.
4. Drop Mode is for the sharpest limited-time finds.
5. If the deal is good, it should feel live.

### 5 ad hooks

1. Stop scrolling stale coupon lists.
2. The best local deals are not meant to sit around.
3. This app shows what is live near you right now.
4. Want better online drops without the mess?
5. Your city has better deals than you think.

### 5 creator prompts

1. Find the best coffee or lunch deal near campus in under 10 minutes.
2. Compare one normal shopping run versus one LiveDrop run.
3. Test Drop Mode and show the best online finds in one session.
4. Build a cheap date night using only LiveDrop offers.
5. Make a neighborhood deal guide for one busy street or district.

## Launch Week Plan

### Week 0: setup

- Seed one target city or neighborhood with enough quality deals to feel alive
- Prepare 10 TikToks, 7 reels, 10 story frames, 3 Reddit posts
- Recruit 10 to 20 local businesses for pilot drops

### Week 1: launch

- Publish daily short-form content across TikTok and Instagram
- Post 2 to 3 useful Reddit threads in relevant communities
- DM and visit local businesses every day
- Repost every real user screenshot, claim, or merchant win

### Week 2 to 4: tighten

- Double down on best-performing hook formats
- Turn top merchant results into proof-based content
- Focus outbound on categories already showing conversion
- Build repeat local content around one city before expanding

## Low-Cost Growth Tactics

- Founder face plus screen recording is enough for launch content
- Use real nearby deals as the creative, not abstract brand ads
- Offer merchants free setup instead of paid discounts
- Incentivize UGC by reposting "best drop I caught today" content
- Ask users to share deal screenshots to stories and tag LiveDrop
- Build one strong city pocket before broad expansion

## Success Metrics

### Consumer

- New installs by channel
- Cost per install if any paid spend is used
- Daily active users
- Deal views per user
- Claim or click-through rate on live deals

### Business

- Number of active merchants
- Drops posted per merchant per week
- Repeat merchant usage
- Merchant feedback on traffic quality

## What To Avoid

- Overpromising scale before city density exists
- Generic "save money" language with no live context
- Spammy urgency words in every asset
- Wide paid acquisition before local supply is healthy
- Overproduced creative that looks unlike the product

## Recommended First Move

Pick one city zone, recruit 10 strong local merchants, publish daily TikTok and Instagram content built from real offers, and use that proof to power Reddit posts and direct business outreach. Density and proof matter more than broad reach at this stage.
